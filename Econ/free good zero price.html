學生提問 (Student's Question):

為甚麼免費物品的定義要特別強調「價格為零時」？

Why does the definition of free goods specifically emphasize "when the price is zero"?

1. 移除「價格」這個限制 (Removing the "Price" Barrier)

在現實世界中，價格 (Price) 是一種分配資源的機制。當一件物品有價格（即 \(P > 0\)）時，人們的「需求量」會因為需要付錢而被壓抑或限制。

In the real world, Price acts as a mechanism to allocate resources. When a good has a price (i.e., \(P > 0\)), people's "quantity demanded" is suppressed or restricted because they have to pay for it.

要真正測試一件物品是否「稀少 (Scarce)」，我們必須移除價格這個障礙。我們假設這件物品是完全免費任拿的（即價格為零）。如果在這種極端情況下，現有數量依然足夠滿足所有人的慾望，這件物品才是真正的「免費物品 (Free Good)」。

To truly test whether a good is "scarce", we must remove the price barrier. We assume the good is completely free for anyone to take as much as they want (i.e., at zero price). If, under this extreme condition, the available quantity is still sufficient to satisfy everyone's wants, only then is the good a true "Free Good".

2. 經濟學的邏輯推演 (Economic Logic Deduction)

我們用需求量 \(Q_d\) (Quantity Demanded) 和現有數量 \(Q_s\) (Quantity Available/Supplied) 來表達：

We express this using Quantity Demanded \(Q_d\) and Quantity Available/Supplied \(Q_s\):

測試條件：當 \(P = 0\) 時 (Test Condition: When \(P = 0\)) $$ \text{If } Q_d > Q_s \implies \text{Economic Good (經濟物品)} $$ $$ \text{If } Q_d \le Q_s \implies \text{Free Good (免費物品)} $$

3. 生活例子對比 (Real-world Examples)

例子一：空氣 (Air) - 免費物品
當空氣的價格為零（\(P=0\)），每個人都可以盡情呼吸。即使大家拼命吸氣（極大的 \(Q_d\)），地球上的空氣總量（\(Q_s\)）依然綽綽有餘。因為 \(Q_s > Q_d\)，所以空氣是免費物品。

Example 1: Air - Free Good
When the price of air is zero (\(P=0\)), everyone can breathe as much as they want. Even if everyone breathes heavily (huge \(Q_d\)), the total amount of air on Earth (\(Q_s\)) is still more than enough. Because \(Q_s > Q_d\), air is a free good.

例子二：最新款 iPhone - 經濟物品
假設蘋果公司今天宣佈 iPhone「價格為零」（免費派發）。你可以想像，全世界的人都會去排隊，想要拿10部、100部（\(Q_d\) 趨近無限大）。但蘋果的庫存（\(Q_s\)）是有限的。因為在 \(P=0\) 時，\(Q_d > Q_s\)，這證明了 iPhone 具有稀少性，是經濟物品。

Example 2: The latest iPhone - Economic Good
Suppose Apple announces today that iPhones are "at zero price" (given away for free). You can imagine everyone in the world queuing up, wanting 10 or 100 phones (\(Q_d\) approaches infinity). But Apple's inventory (\(Q_s\)) is limited. Because at \(P=0\), \(Q_d > Q_s\), this proves the iPhone is scarce and is an economic good.

總結 (Conclusion)

強調「當價格為零時」，是為了創造一個沒有金錢限制的測試環境，從而暴露出該物品是否真正具有「稀少性 (Scarcity)」。

Emphasizing "at zero price" is to create a test environment without financial constraints, thereby exposing whether the good truly possesses "Scarcity".
