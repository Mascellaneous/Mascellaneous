經濟學深度解析：機會成本與決策

Topic: Opportunity Cost (機會成本)
Difficulty: ★★★☆☆
Key Concepts: Monetary Cost, Non-monetary Cost, Full Cost

The Question (題目)

假設有兩個選項：
選項1：以 \(\$a\) 賣出舊電話，再以 \(\$b\) 買一部新電話
選項2：繼續使用舊電話

問題：選擇以舊換新(選項1)的金錢成本、非金錢成本、全成本是多少？

Suppose there are two options:
Option 1: Sell the old phone for \(\$a\), and buy a new phone for \(\$b\).
Option 2: Continue using the old phone.
Question: What is the monetary cost, non-monetary cost, and full cost of choosing Option 1 (trade-in)?

Concept Review (概念重溫)

在經濟學中，機會成本 (Opportunity Cost) 被定義為「被放棄的選項中價值最高者」(The highest-valued option forgone)。當我們作出選擇時，必須考慮所有付出的代價，這包括實際支付的金錢，以及放棄的其他非金錢價值。

In economics, Opportunity Cost is defined as "the highest-valued option forgone." When making a choice, we must consider all sacrifices made, which include both the actual money paid and the non-monetary value given up.
  • 金錢成本 (Monetary Cost / Explicit Cost): 決策中涉及的實際淨現金流出。
  • 非金錢成本 (Non-monetary Cost / Implicit Cost): 決策中放棄的非金錢價值（例如時間、物品的使用價值）。
  • Monetary Cost: The actual net cash outflow involved in a decision.
  • Non-monetary Cost: The non-monetary value given up in a decision (e.g., time, use value of a good).
$$ \text{Full Cost (全成本)} = \text{Monetary Cost (金錢成本)} + \text{Non-monetary Cost (非金錢成本)} $$

The Answer & Detailed Analysis (答案與詳細分析)

要計算選擇「選項1」的成本，我們必須比較「選項1」與「選項2」之間的差異。選擇選項1意味著你放棄了選項2。

To calculate the cost of choosing "Option 1", we must compare the differences between "Option 1" and "Option 2". Choosing Option 1 means you give up Option 2.

1. 金錢成本 (Monetary Cost) = \(\$(b - a)\)

分析：當你選擇選項1時，你需要支付 \(\$b\) 來購買新電話。然而，這個行動同時包含了「賣出舊電話」這個動作，為你帶來了 \(\$a\) 的現金收入。因此，你的淨現金流出是 \(\$(b - a)\)。這 \(\$a\) 抵銷了部分買新電話的支出。

Analysis: When you choose Option 1, you need to pay \(\$b\) to buy the new phone. However, this action inherently includes "selling the old phone," which brings you a cash inflow of \(\$a\). Therefore, your net cash outflow is \(\$(b - a)\). This \(\$a\) offsets part of the expenditure for the new phone.

2. 非金錢成本 (Non-monetary Cost) = 繼續使用舊電話的價值 (Use value of the old phone)

分析：選擇選項1的代價不僅僅是金錢。因為你賣掉了舊電話，你失去了原本可以「繼續使用舊電話」所帶來的效用（Utility）或享受。這是一個非金錢的犧牲。

Analysis: The sacrifice of choosing Option 1 is not just monetary. Because you sold the old phone, you lose the utility or enjoyment that would have come from "continuing to use the old phone." This is a non-monetary sacrifice.

3. 全成本 (Full Cost) = \(\$(b - a)\) + 繼續使用舊電話的價值

分析：將上述兩者相加，就是選擇以舊換新的全部機會成本。

Analysis: Adding the two components above gives the total opportunity cost of choosing the trade-in option.

Problem Decomposition (題目拆解)

這類題目的關鍵轉折點 (Pivot Point) 在於如何處理 \(\$a\)（賣出舊電話的收入）。

很多學生會把 \(\$a\) 視為「放棄的收入」，但請仔細看題目：選項1是「賣出舊電話並買新電話」。這意味著如果你選擇選項1，你確實會收到 \(\$a\)。因此，\(\$a\) 不是你放棄的東西，而是你獲得的補償，它會降低你的金錢成本。

The Pivot Point of this type of question lies in how to handle \(\$a\) (the revenue from selling the old phone).
Many students might treat \(\$a\) as "forgone income", but look closely at the question: Option 1 is "Sell the old phone AND buy a new phone". This means if you choose Option 1, you actually receive \(\$a\). Therefore, \(\$a\) is not something you give up; it is a compensation you receive, which reduces your monetary cost.

Deep Dive & Underlying Confusion (深度探討與盲點分析)

為什麼這題容易讓人混淆？
這題觸及了經濟學中一個非常核心的認知陷阱：將「資產的市場價值」與「資產的使用價值」混為一談。

在選項2（繼續使用舊電話）中，舊電話有兩個潛在價值：

  1. 把它賣掉能換取的金錢 (\(\$a\))
  2. 自己使用的效用 (Use value)
你不能同時擁有兩者。當你選擇選項1時，你選擇了獲取 \(\$a\)（這降低了買新機的成本），但你必須放棄「自己使用的效用」。這就是為什麼非金錢成本是「使用舊電話的價值」，而不是 \(\$a\)。

Why is this confusing?
This question touches upon a core cognitive trap in economics: confusing the "market value of an asset" with the "use value of an asset."
In Option 2 (keeping the old phone), the old phone has two potential values:
  1. The money it can be sold for (\(\$a\))
  2. The utility of using it yourself (Use value)
You cannot have both simultaneously. When you choose Option 1, you choose to acquire \(\$a\) (which lowers the cost of buying the new phone), but you must give up the "utility of using it yourself." This is why the non-monetary cost is the "use value of the old phone," not \(\$a\).

Common Mistakes & Traps (常犯錯誤與陷阱)

陷阱 1：認為金錢成本是 \(\$b\)。
錯誤原因：忽略了賣出舊電話帶來的現金回流。在經濟學中，我們計算的是「淨」金錢代價 (Net monetary cost)。

Trap 1: Thinking the monetary cost is \(\$b\).
Reason for error: Ignoring the cash inflow from selling the old phone. In economics, we calculate the "net" monetary cost.

陷阱 2：加入舊電話當初的買入價 (Historical Cost)。
錯誤原因：舊電話當初買入的價格是沉沒成本 (Sunk Cost)。無論你現在選擇選項1還是選項2，這筆錢都無法收回，因此不應計入機會成本中。

Trap 2: Including the original purchase price of the old phone.
Reason for error: The original purchase price is a Sunk Cost. Whether you choose Option 1 or Option 2 now, this money cannot be recovered, so it should not be included in the opportunity cost.

Student Q&A (學生常見疑問)

Q1: 如果題目沒有說「賣出舊電話」，只是說「買一部新電話」，成本會有什麼不同？

A: 如果選項1只是「買一部新電話」（而你保留舊電話），那麼金錢成本就是 \(\$b\)。因為你沒有賣出舊電話，所以沒有 \(\$a\) 來抵銷支出。同時，你也沒有放棄舊電話的使用價值（因為你保留了它），所以非金錢成本中不會包含「放棄使用舊電話的價值」。

A: If Option 1 was simply "buy a new phone" (and you keep the old one), the monetary cost would be \(\$b\). Since you didn't sell the old phone, there is no \(\$a\) to offset the expenditure. Also, you haven't given up the use value of the old phone (since you kept it), so the non-monetary cost would not include "the forgone use value of the old phone."
Q2: 我們怎麼知道「繼續使用舊電話的價值」大於還是小於 \(\$a\)？

A: 題目並沒有提供足夠數據來比較兩者。但在現實決策中，如果消費者認為「新電話的效用」大於「\(\$(b - a)\) 加上舊電話的使用價值」，他就會選擇以舊換新。這屬於邊際分析與理性選擇的範疇。

A: The question doesn't provide enough data to compare the two. But in real-world decision-making, if a consumer believes the "utility of the new phone" is greater than "\(\$(b - a)\) plus the use value of the old phone," they will choose to trade in. This falls under marginal analysis and rational choice.
